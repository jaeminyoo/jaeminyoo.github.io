Jaemin Yoo

Postdoctoral Researcher at CMU

I am a postdoctoral research fellow at Carnegie Mellon University working with Prof. Leman Akoglu. I received my Ph.D. and B.S. in Computer Science and Engineering from Seoul National University, where I was advised by Prof. U Kang. My doctoral studies were supported by the Google PhD Fellowship and the Qualcomm Innovation Fellowship. I am a recipient of the Outstanding Dissertation Awards from Seoul National University and the Korean Academy of Science and Technology (KAST).

The focus of my research is to design graph neural networks (GNNs) that are robust and generalizable to real-world graphs. GNNs face the challenge of incomplete and insufficient data in many real-world applications, resulting in either the failure of training or poor generalization to unseen data. I am committed to designing a systematic approach that can effectively address these practical challenges without relying on data-specific parameter tuning. While most of my research focuses on data that is already represented as a graph, I am also interested in exploring ways to extract relationships from non-graphical data.
